## 2) What Ecuadorian law firm websites in Quito typically look like

In the Quito/Ecuador market, smaller and mid-size law firm websites usually lead with **direct contact options** and a practical service menu, not abstract corporate language. Across examples I reviewed, common navigation patterns are **Inicio, Sobre Nosotros / Nuestra Empresa, Servicios or Áreas, Equipo / Perfiles, Blog / Noticias, and Contacto**. Smaller firms often list concrete practice areas such as **familia, penal, tránsito, laboral, civil, administrativo y notariales**, while larger firms add **publications** and regulatory updates. ([Ag Abogados][5])

The tone is usually **formal but close**, emphasizing **ética, compromiso, experiencia, claridad, confianza, patrocinio integral** and realistic solutions. This is very different from a US big-law template. Ecuadorian sites often position the lawyer as someone who will **acompañar, orientar, representar y patrocinar** the client through the process, not just “provide legal services.” ([Carrillo & Asociados][6])

It also matters that Ecuadorian legal language is grounded in local institutions and procedure. The **Consejo de la Judicatura** exposes online services such as **SATJE** and **SUPA**; the **Fiscalía General del Estado** leads criminal investigation; and the **COGEP** framework emphasizes **oral hearings / oralidad** in non-penal matters. So copy that mentions **audiencias, patrocinio, notificaciones electrónicas, SATJE, SUPA, Fiscalía, mediación** and practical trámites will feel much more native to the Ecuadorian market. ([Función Judicial][7])

# 4) Feature suggestions, cost, and recommendation

## Simple features for a static site

**WhatsApp click-to-chat**
What it does: opens a WhatsApp conversation immediately from the website. WhatsApp officially supports click-to-chat and direct links, and WhatsApp Business itself is free to download for small businesses. This is a must-have for a small Ecuadorian firm. ([WhatsApp Help Center][8])
Cost: **Free**.
Recommendation: **Strongly recommend.** Make this the main CTA.

**Click-to-call phone link**
What it does: mobile users tap the number and call instantly.
Cost: **Free**.
Recommendation: **Strongly recommend.** Very low effort and important for legal-services conversion.

**Google Maps embed**
What it does: shows the office location and helps clients navigate. Google’s official Maps Embed documentation says the Maps Embed API is free to use with unlimited requests, though the official API flow requires an API key. ([Google for Developers][9])
Cost: **Free** in practice for this use case.
Recommendation: **Recommend.** Especially important in Quito where intersection-based addresses are common.

**Contact form using Formspree or EmailJS**
What it does: lets clients send a message from the site without a backend. Formspree is built for HTML/static forms and explicitly works with static sites and GitHub-style deployments; EmailJS also works without server code and its free plan shows **200 monthly requests**. ([formspree.io][10])
Cost: **Free tier available** on both.
Recommendation: **Recommend Formspree over EmailJS** for this project. It is usually cleaner for a simple law-firm intake form.

**Social media links**
What it does: adds Facebook, Instagram and other trust signals.
Cost: **Free**.
Recommendation: **Recommend Facebook and WhatsApp first.** Add Instagram only if they will post consistently. TikTok only makes sense if they truly plan short legal explainer videos.

**FAQ section**
What it does: answers common pre-consultation questions like divorce, alimentos, despido, tránsito, honorarios, and required documents.
Cost: **Free**.
Recommendation: **Recommend.** It improves SEO and reduces repetitive WhatsApp questions.

## Medium-complexity features with third-party integrations

**WhatsApp chatbot**
What it does: replies automatically to common questions. For a small business, WhatsApp Business already offers greeting messages and quick replies; full automation platforms such as Wati are paid and add ongoing setup/admin work. ([WhatsApp Help Center][11])
Cost: **Free** if you only use WhatsApp Business quick replies; **paid** if you use API/chatbot tools.
Recommendation: **Do not start with a full chatbot.** Start with WhatsApp Business quick replies only.

**Appointment scheduling**
What it does: lets clients book a consultation slot themselves. Google Calendar supports appointment schedules and embeddable booking pages; Calendly has a free tier; Cal.com says individuals can start for free with unlimited scheduling. ([Google Help][12])
Cost: **Free tier available**.
Recommendation: **Recommend one simple booking page**, not a complex multi-lawyer routing system. For this project, one “Consulta inicial” calendar is the most practical. Only create separate calendars per lawyer if each lawyer will truly manage their own availability.

**Live chat widget**
What it does: adds website chat for visitors who do not want to call or use WhatsApp. tawk.to states its software is **truly 100% free** when you answer the chats yourself. ([tawk.to][13])
Cost: **Free**.
Recommendation: **Conditionally recommend.** Only install it if someone will actually answer. A silent chat widget hurts trust.

**Google Reviews integration**
What it does: displays reviews from the firm’s Google Business Profile on the website. In practice this usually needs a widget provider; Elfsight offers a Google Reviews widget with a free plan and automatic updates. ([Elfsight][14])
Cost: **Usually free-to-paid depending on widget limits**.
Recommendation: **Recommend later**, after the firm has a properly set up Google Business Profile and a few genuine reviews.

## Complex features that are probably not worth it here

**Per-lawyer availability by case type with backend calendar sync**
What it does: routes “familia” to one lawyer, “penal” to another, based on live availability.
Cost: **Paid + setup + maintenance**.
Recommendation: **Not worth it** for a small Ecuadorian practice unless the team becomes much larger.

**Client portal with case tracking**
What it does: clients log in to see filings, hearing dates, documents, and updates.
Cost: **Paid / custom development**.
Recommendation: **Not worth it** for a GitHub Pages static site project.

**Online payment integration**
What it does: charges consultation or service fees online.
Cost: **Paid / compliance / admin overhead**.
Recommendation: **Usually not worth it at launch** unless they plan prepaid consultations from day one.

**Document upload system**
What it does: clients upload IDs, contracts, demanda papers, evidence, etc.
Cost: **Paid / storage / privacy / security burden**.
Recommendation: **Not worth it** for launch. Ask clients to send documents through controlled channels after first contact.
