## 2. How Ecuadorian law firms present themselves online

An analysis of **15+ law firm websites in Quito** — ranging from major corporate firms like Bustamante Fabara and Fexlaw to small consumer-facing practices like Robelly & Asociados and AbogadosQuito.vip — reveals clear patterns that the Consorcio Jurídico HP website should follow.

### Common website structure

Nearly every Ecuadorian law firm website follows this navigation pattern:

- **Inicio** (Home) — Hero section with tagline, counters (years of experience, cases handled), and primary CTA
- **Nosotros / Quiénes Somos** (About) — Firm history, mission, values
- **Áreas de Práctica / Servicios** (Practice areas) — Card-based layout with brief descriptions; sometimes individual sub-pages
- **Equipo / Nuestro Equipo** (Team) — Professional headshots with credentials
- **Contacto / Contáctanos** (Contact) — Phone, email, WhatsApp, Google Maps embed, physical address with building/floor details
- **Blog / Noticias** (optional but common for medium firms) — Legal updates, SEO content

Less common but notable additions include testimonial sections, client logo carousels, privacy policy pages (citing Ecuador's LOPDP), and "primera consulta gratis" (free first consultation) badges.

### Two distinct tones exist in the market

**Large/corporate firms** (Bustamante Fabara, DGA Legal, Fexlaw) use highly formal, institutional language, are bilingual Spanish/English, emphasize international rankings (Chambers, Legal 500), and project corporate prestige with navy/gold color palettes.

**Small/consumer-facing firms** — the category Consorcio Jurídico HP falls into — use more approachable language, emotional appeals like "Luchamos por tus derechos" and "Tu tranquilidad es nuestra prioridad," offer free first consultations, and make **WhatsApp the primary call-to-action**. They use "usted" for formality but maintain warmth. Common vocabulary includes: *"enfoque personalizado," "experiencia y compromiso," "soluciones legales," "ética profesional," "transparencia," "defensa de sus derechos."*

### What distinguishes Ecuadorian law firm sites from US/European templates

- **WhatsApp dominates.** Floating WhatsApp buttons are the primary CTA, far more prominent than email or phone. Pre-filled messages like "Hola, necesito asesoría legal" are standard.
- **Derecho de Tránsito** (traffic law) appears as a standalone practice area — virtually unseen on US/EU sites.
- **Counter animations** are ubiquitous: years of experience, cases won, satisfaction percentages.
- **Physical office addresses** include building names, floor numbers, and Google Maps — reflecting Ecuador's culture of in-person meetings.
- **"Consorcio Jurídico," "Bufete," "Estudio Jurídico"** are the local firm-type terms ("Estudio Jurídico" is most prestigious; "Bufete" is common; "Firma" is corporate).
- **Price sensitivity** — some firms openly advertise consultation fees ($30) or free first consultations.
- **WordPress with Elementor** is the dominant tech stack for small and medium firms.
- **Color palettes** lean toward navy blue (authority), white (clean), and gold accents (prestige). Dark themes signal modernity.

### Recommended structure for Consorcio Jurídico HP

The site should include: **Inicio, Quiénes Somos, Áreas de Práctica (with expandable sections or sub-pages), Nuestro Equipo, Testimonios, and Contacto.** A Blog/Noticias Jurídicas page would help with SEO. Every page should have a floating WhatsApp button and a visible phone number. The hero section should feature a tagline like "Soluciones legales con compromiso y experiencia," counter animations (años de experiencia, casos atendidos, clientes satisfechos), and a "Consulte ahora" CTA linking to WhatsApp.

## 3. Legal practice areas for the Ecuadorian market

Ecuador operates under a **civil law tradition** governed by written codes and statutes. The 2008 Constitution of Montecristi is the supreme norm. Two procedural pillars define modern practice: the **COIP** (Código Orgánico Integral Penal, 2014) governs criminal and traffic matters, while the **COGEP** (Código Orgánico General de Procesos, 2016) introduced oral hearings for all non-criminal litigation — a historic transformation from the old written system.

Below are the 12 practice areas most relevant for the firm, each contextualized for Ecuador's legal system.

### Derecho Penal (criminal law)

Governed by the **COIP**, which unified substantive criminal law, criminal procedure, and penitentiary law into one code. Criminal proceedings follow the sequence: investigación previa → instrucción fiscal → evaluación y preparatoria de juicio → juicio, all through **oral hearings**. The **Fiscalía General del Estado** directs all criminal investigations and holds exclusive power over public criminal action. Common cases include robbery, fraud (estafa), domestic violence, drug offenses, and organized crime. Defense attorneys appear before Unidades Judiciales Penales and Tribunales de Garantías Penales. Ecuador's ongoing security crisis has sharply increased demand for criminal defense.

### Derecho de Familia, Niñez y Adolescencia (family law)

One of the highest-demand areas. Ecuador processes **17,000+ divorces annually**. Key legislation includes the Código Civil (Books I–III), the Código de la Niñez y Adolescencia, and the COGEP (procedimiento sumario). Divorce by mutual consent without minor children can be processed at a **notaría** — no court required. Contested divorces require proving legal grounds (abandonment over 6 months, violence, adultery). **Pensiones alimenticias** (child support) are calculated per an official table set by the Consejo de la Judicatura based on income, number of children, and ages; **80% of awards fall under $125/month**. Non-payment can trigger *apremio personal* (arrest). Cases go before Unidades Judiciales de Familia, Mujer, Niñez y Adolescencia. The **SUPA system** (Sistema Único de Pensiones Alimenticias) digitally manages support payments.

### Derecho Laboral (labor law)

Governed by the **Código del Trabajo** (private sector) and **LOSEP** (public sector). Claims follow the procedimiento sumario (single audiencia única). The most common claim is **despido intempestivo** (unfair dismissal), with indemnification of up to 3 months' salary (under 3 years of service) or 1 month per year (over 3 years, max 25 months). Key procedures include the *visto bueno* (administrative termination via the Inspector del Trabajo) and *despido ineficaz* (special protection for pregnant women, union leaders, and persons with disabilities). Workers are also entitled to décimo tercer sueldo, décimo cuarto sueldo, 15% profit sharing (utilidades), and vacation. The **Ministerio de Trabajo** and **IESS** (Instituto Ecuatoriano de Seguridad Social) are the key institutions.

### Derecho Civil (civil law)

Covers contracts, obligations, property, torts (responsabilidad civil), and inheritance. The Código Civil's four books address persons, property, successions, and obligations/contracts. Under the COGEP, complex civil cases follow the **procedimiento ordinario** (two hearings: preliminar + juicio), while simpler disputes use the **procedimiento sumario** (single hearing). Common matters include real estate transactions, rental disputes (Ley de Inquilinato), inheritance proceedings (posesión efectiva, partición de bienes), and prescription claims. Notarías handle non-contentious matters; the Registro de la Propiedad and Registro Mercantil are key registries.

### Derecho de Tránsito (traffic and transportation law)

A uniquely prominent practice area in Ecuador due to high accident rates. Since 2014, traffic infractions are codified within the **COIP** (Articles 371–392). Traffic *contravenciones* (minor infractions) are processed by judges of contravenciones through oral hearings. Traffic *delitos* (cases involving death or serious injury) follow full criminal procedure. The **ANT** (Agencia Nacional de Tránsito) regulates licensing, while **SIAT** (Sistema de Investigación de Accidentes de Tránsito) handles crash investigations. The **SPPAT** (Sistema Público para Pago de Accidentes de Tránsito) is the mandatory accident insurance fund. Drunk driving cases, accident claims, and license-point disputes are extremely common.

### Derecho Societario / Corporativo (corporate and business law)

Governed by the **Ley de Compañías**, which was significantly reformed in March 2023 to simplify business formation. Companies (Cía. Ltda. and S.A.) can now be constituted with a private document rather than a public deed. The **Superintendencia de Compañías, Valores y Seguros** is the primary regulatory body. Common services include company incorporation (including the new SAS — Sociedad por Acciones Simplificada), capital increases, mergers, dissolutions, shareholder disputes, commercial contracts, and regulatory compliance. The **SRI** (Servicio de Rentas Internas) handles tax registration. Quito, as Ecuador's economic center, generates constant demand.

### Cobranzas Judiciales (judicial debt collection)

A high-demand area transformed by the COGEP. Two key procedures exist: the **procedimiento ejecutivo** (for debts backed by a título ejecutivo — pagarés, letras de cambio, protested checks, court judgments) requires two hearings; the **procedimiento monitorio** (for debts up to 50 SBU without a título ejecutivo) is a COGEP innovation that dramatically simplified small debt collection. If the debtor fails to appear, the judge proceeds directly to execution. Enforcement mechanisms include embargo, retención, and prohibición de enajenar bienes.

### Derecho Administrativo (administrative law)

Regulates citizen-versus-State disputes. The **COA** (Código Orgánico Administrativo, 2017) created a unified framework for administrative procedures. Contentious-administrative claims follow the procedimiento ordinario under COGEP before the **Tribunales de lo Contencioso Administrativo**. Key actions include the recurso subjetivo (for individual rights violations), recurso objetivo (for annulment of illegal acts), and challenges to administrative silence (silencio administrativo). Relevant institutions include the Procuraduría General del Estado, Contraloría General del Estado, and SERCOP for public procurement disputes.

### Derecho Constitucional / Garantías Jurisdiccionales

Ecuador's 2008 Constitution created a robust system of constitutional protections. **Any judge** can hear garantías jurisdiccionales. The key actions are: **Acción de Protección** (Article 88, protects all constitutional rights against acts of public authorities or certain private parties), **Hábeas Corpus** (Article 89, protects liberty), **Hábeas Data** (Article 92, personal data access/correction), and **Acción de Acceso a la Información Pública** (Article 91). These are summary, oral proceedings with hearings within 24–48 hours. The **Corte Constitucional del Ecuador** reviews all constitutional actions.

### Derecho Tributario (tax law)

Covers disputes with the SRI (Servicio de Rentas Internas) over tax assessments, audits (glosas), fines, and claims for undue or excess payments. The Código Tributario and Ley de Régimen Tributario Interno are the key codes. Contentious tax claims follow the procedimiento ordinario under COGEP before specialized Tribunales Distritales de lo Fiscal.

### Derecho Inmobiliario (real estate law)

Covers property transactions, leases, mortgages, condominium law (Ley de Propiedad Horizontal), easements, and construction disputes. All real estate sales require an escritura pública (public deed) and registration at the Registro de la Propiedad. Evictions follow procedimiento sumario. Prescription claims (prescripción adquisitiva/usucapión) for property acquired through long possession are common.

### Derecho Migratorio (immigration law)

Governed by the **Ley Orgánica de Movilidad Humana (LOMHU)**. Covers visa applications, residency permits, naturalization, deportation defense, and foreign title recognition. The Ministerio de Relaciones Exteriores y Movilidad Humana is the key institution. Ecuador has seen significant immigration (particularly Venezuelan migration), making this an increasingly relevant practice area.

## 4. Free tools for the website

### WhatsApp integration — the highest priority

WhatsApp is Ecuador's dominant communication channel, making this the single most important website feature.

**WhatsApp Business App** (free) is the right choice for a small firm — it includes a business profile, automated greeting messages, away messages, quick replies, labels for organizing chats, and broadcast messaging to up to 256 contacts. The API version is overkill and costly.

The **click-to-chat link** for the firm is: `https://wa.me/593993646082` (country code +593, drop the leading 0). With a pre-filled message: `https://wa.me/593993646082?text=Hola%2C%20necesito%20una%20consulta%20legal`.

For a **floating WhatsApp button** on the website, the best free options are **GetButton.io** (free with branding, multi-messenger support, no coding required) and **OpenWidget** (100% free, bundles WhatsApp + Google Reviews + contact form + FAQ in one widget). Both support custom Spanish messages. Elfsight offers a WhatsApp widget but limits free-tier views to approximately 200/month.

### Appointment scheduling — Cal.com wins

**Cal.com** (free plan) is the strongest option. It offers **unlimited event types, unlimited calendar connections, and unlimited bookings** — all free. This means the firm can create separate booking types for "Consulta Inicial," "Seguimiento," and "Revisión de Documentos." It supports Spanish, integrates easily via embed code, and even includes workflow automation for reminders. By comparison, **Calendly's free plan** limits users to just 1 active event type and 1 calendar connection. Google Calendar's built-in scheduling works but looks less professional.

### Live chat — Tawk.to is unmatched

**Tawk.to** is 100% free with no limits: unlimited agents, unlimited chat volume, unlimited websites. It includes real-time visitor monitoring, a ticketing system, a knowledge base, canned responses, pre-chat forms, and mobile apps. It **supports Spanish natively** (one of 49 languages) for the entire widget interface. Integration is a single JavaScript snippet pasted before the `</body>` tag, and a dedicated WordPress plugin with 5M+ installs exists. The only cost is optional: removing "Powered by tawk.to" branding ($29/month) or adding AI Assist.

### Contact forms — Formspree for simplicity

**Formspree** (free tier: 50 submissions/month, unlimited forms) requires only an HTML `action` attribute — no JavaScript, no backend. This is more than sufficient for a small firm. Forms are written in Spanish by the developer. If volume exceeds 50/month, **EmailJS** (200 emails/month free) is an alternative but requires basic JavaScript. For WordPress sites, **Contact Form 7** or **WPForms Lite** are free plugins that handle unlimited submissions.

### Google Reviews — build credibility locally

First, **create a Google Business Profile** at google.com/business (free). Select category "Abogado" or "Bufete de abogados," add the Quito address, phone number, business hours, Spanish-language description, and office photos. Verification typically requires a postcard mailed to the office. Once verified, the firm appears in Google's local map pack when someone searches "abogado en Quito."

To **embed Google Reviews on the website**, the best free options are **EmbedSocial** (free forever plan, uses the official Google Business API, auto-syncs reviews, multiple layouts) and **OpenWidget** (bundles reviews with WhatsApp and contact form). Both use simple embed codes.

### WhatsApp chatbot — not needed yet

A dedicated chatbot is unnecessary for a small firm. The **WhatsApp Business App's built-in features** cover 90% of needs: automated greetings ("¡Bienvenido! Somos Consorcio Jurídico HP. ¿En qué podemos ayudarle?"), away messages ("Estamos fuera de horario. Le responderemos mañana a las 9am."), and quick replies ("/servicios" → list of practice areas, "/horario" → business hours). True chatbot platforms (ManyChat, Landbot, Respond.io) all require paid WhatsApp API access starting at $15–79/month — disproportionate for this stage.
